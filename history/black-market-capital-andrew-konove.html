In this extraordinary new book, Andrew Konove traces the history of illicit commerce in Mexico City and how it became central to the economy and politics of the city from the seventeenth century onward. The story is centered on the untold history of the Baratillo, the city’s most infamous thieves’ market, which originated in the colonial-era Plaza Mayor and moved to the neighborhood of Tepito, where in the twentieth century it grew into one of the world’s largest black-market emporiums. Konove uncovers the far-reaching financial and political ties between vendors in the Baratillo and government officials, newspaper publishers, and mercantile elites in Mexico City, revealing the surprising clout of vendors who trafficked in the shadow economy.
